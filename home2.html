The Tax Lien Opportunity Overview

Did you know counties across the United States collect billions in unpaid real estate taxes annually? Learn how buying these debts grants you secured legal interest.

How Counties Guarantee Yields

Unlike stocks, tax liens are fully collateralized by real property. If the property owner pays, you receive 8% to 36% annual interest rates established by county statute.

If they do not pay within the redemption window, you foreclose. Through this mechanism, investors frequently acquire properties worth hundreds of thousands of dollars for pennies on the dollar.

Risk Management

Critical Mistakes to avoid in auctions

Tax auctions are caveat emptor (buyer beware). Here are top reasons novice auction bidders lose money without expert oversight.

1. Bidding on Industrial Wasteland

Securing a tax lien on contaminated commercial property forces environmental liabilities onto the foreclosing investor. Remediation costs often exceed land valuation.

2. Ignoring Prior IRS Tax Liens

While local property taxes supersede mortgages, Federal IRS tax liens hold special redemption statuses. Ignoring federal encumbrances risks losing clear title path.

3. Buying Inaccessible Land Parcels

Counties frequently auction certificates on slivers of land, land-locked areas, or public easements. We verify geographic physical access before every bid.
